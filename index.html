What is happening?

Global farmers face challenges from climate change, extreme weather, and supply chain disruptions, including those heightened by the COVID-19 pandemic. Changing temperatures affect crop suitability, while technological advancements like precision farming offer efficiency but require adaptation. To address these complexities, farmers are increasingly adopting sustainable practices such as regenerative agriculture to build resilience against climate-related shocks. Balancing innovation and sustainability is crucial for the long-term viability of agriculture amid evolving global conditions.

Why is it happening?

The impending food scarcity crisis in 2050 is primarily driven by a growing global population, estimated to reach 9.7 billion people. This surge in population will intensify the demand for food, placing immense pressure on agricultural systems. Climate change, water scarcity, and soil degradation further exacerbate the challenges, impacting crop yields and making sustainable food production more difficult. Urgent efforts are needed to address these issues through sustainable agricultural practices, technological innovations, and global cooperation to ensure food security for the growing population.
